# Impeccable Design Context

## Users

**Primary:** Hiring managers and engineering leads at top-tier tech companies evaluating senior frontend candidates. They've seen hundreds of portfolios. They scan in 3 seconds, judge in 10. They're looking for proof of craft, not claims.

**Secondary:** Potential SaaS clients or collaborators who need someone who can ship production-grade UI. They care about taste and execution speed.

**Context:** Visiting from LinkedIn, a job board, or a direct referral. Usually on desktop. Time-poor, pattern-matching for signals of seniority.

## Brand Personality

**Creative, Experimental, Expressive**

The site is a frontend engineering showpiece — the medium IS the message. Every interaction should make visitors wonder "how did they build this?" The personality is confident but not boastful, technical but not sterile, expressive but disciplined.

**Voice:** First-person, direct, no corporate jargon. "I built" not "solutions were delivered." Mono-spaced labels for technical credibility, serif for personality, sans-serif for body.

**Emotional target:** Technical awe in the first 3 seconds. The visitor should feel they're experiencing something crafted, not generated.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Dark, spatial, cinematic. Depth through layering — video atmosphere, floating 3D panels, parallax trust chips, particle systems. The dark theme (#04040a) is non-negotiable; it's the canvas that makes the purple/cyan gradient accents glow.

**References (inferred from codebase choices):**
- Apple product pages (scroll-driven reveals, cinematic pacing)
- Linear.app (dark UI, glass morphism, precision)
- Stripe.com (layered depth, gradient craft)

**Anti-references:**
- Generic dev portfolios (cookie-cutter templates, "Hi I'm a developer" energy)
- Over-animated showreels (gratuitous parallax, animations for animation's sake)
- Corporate enterprise (safe, committee-designed, blue-and-white SaaS)
- Brutalist / intentionally raw (no-design aesthetic)

**Theme:** Dark-only. No light mode.

## Design Principles

### 1. The site IS the portfolio
Every interaction, animation, and layout choice should demonstrate frontend craft. If a visitor can't tell this was built by a senior engineer within 3 seconds, the design has failed. The hero section, scroll behavior, and 3D elements are not decoration — they're proof of capability.

### 2. Purposeful motion, never gratuitous
Every animation must earn its place. Scroll-driven reveals build narrative. Parallax creates depth. The video atmosphere sets mood. But nothing moves just because it can. If an animation doesn't serve comprehension, pacing, or delight, remove it. The anti-reference is "over-animated showreel."

### 3. Depth through layering, not clutter
Create spatial richness with z-axis: video background, particle canvas, floating panels, text overlays, glass cards. Each layer has a clear role. The composition should feel three-dimensional and cinematic — like looking into a scene, not at a flat page.

### 4. Dark canvas, luminous accents
The near-black background is the stage. Purple and cyan accents are the performers. Use color sparingly and intentionally — a glowing border, a gradient CTA, a shimmer on text. When everything glows, nothing does.

### 5. Credibility through restraint
Trust chips, stats, and company names should feel matter-of-fact, not boastful. Mono-spaced, small, positioned subtly. The work speaks; the credentials confirm. No exclamation marks, no superlatives, no "passionate about."
